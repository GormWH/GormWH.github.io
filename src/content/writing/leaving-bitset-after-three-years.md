Nearly three years ago I joined bitset as my first job out of engineering school. It was also a pivot: I had spent my undergrad and master's in mechanical engineering, and bitset is where I became a software engineer instead. I chose it because I had no professional experience yet, and a small team where everyone wrote code looked like the fastest way to learn breadth.

It delivered on that. In three years I touched web, desktop apps, embedded work with sensor data, a cloud data-analysis system, a robot fleet control system — more fields than most first jobs ever put in front of you. I am genuinely grateful for that range, and for the people who trusted me with it before I had really earned it.

I am leaving because what I want from a project has shifted. I joined wanting to work on many things, and I did. But the more I built, the more I wanted a hand in deciding what gets built — to spend more of my time on the shape of a project, not only its implementation. bitset gave me real room to suggest features, propose designs, and float ideas; I want that to be a larger part of my work. That is a change in me, not a shortfall in the place.

So this is a thank-you as much as a goodbye. bitset taught me how to be an engineer across a lot of surfaces at once, and I am carrying that range into whatever comes next — just aimed a little further upstream.
