title: "Closer to where the decisions get made"
description: "I moved from a six-person firm to a thousand-person one, and for the first time it feels like someone thought about what work would fit me."
date: 2026-07-06
kind: "Log"
tags: ["Career", "Reflection"]
---

I changed jobs. Both places are in systems integration (SI), so on paper it's the same kind of work — but the two companies sit at very different points in the chain. My old firm had six people and often landed as a subcontractor to a subcontractor, sometimes three tiers deep. That far down, decisions arrive already made; you implement other people's calls and rarely get asked. Even inside the company, when I said what I wanted a hand in or the kind of work I hoped to do, it never seemed to change much.

That is most of why I left. I wanted to do different kinds of work, and if I had stayed I was all but certain to spend the next two or three years on a project I didn't want.

My first assignment is not the one I wanted: a legacy project that needs an upgrade to stay maintainable. Fine — that is the work in front of me. But the thing that made me feel good is what sits behind it. The manager brought up data-analysis projects directly and asked what I wanted to work on. I don't really think in terms of wanting one particular kind of work — I'm more the other sort: if something is worth doing, I want to give it a go. Being asked the question at all is what landed.

That is a small thing and a large thing at once. Someone looked at what I said I could do and asked where I wanted to take it. After years of feeling routed around the decisions, being routed toward one is a change I can feel.
